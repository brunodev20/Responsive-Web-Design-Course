Steve Jobs
Founder of Apple
Here's a time line of Steve Jobs' life:
  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco
  • 1970 - Meets future Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak through a friend
  • 1974 - Begins a brief stint as an engineer at Atari. Working the night shift, he employs Wozniak to help whittle down the hardware required for a prototype of a single-player version of Pong, the game that would go on to become Breakout.
  • 1976 - Co-founds Apple Computer with Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. That same year, the company sells the Apple I in the form of a kit that sells for $666.66.
  • 1980 - Apple goes public, putting Jobs' net worth north of $200 million.
  • 1985 - CEO John Sculley engineers Jobs' ouster from Apple. Jobs resigns as Apple chairman, saying in a board meeting.
  • 1996 - Returns to Apple, as an adviser, after it buys NeXT for $429 million.
  • 1997 - Becomes CEO, initially as the de facto chief, then as interim chief in September.
  • 2001 - Introduces iTunes, then exclusively for Mac users. "iTunes is miles ahead of every other jukebox application, and we hope its dramatically simpler user interface will bring even more people into the digital music revolution."
  • 2004 - Jobs undergoes surgery to remove a cancerous tumor in his pancreas.
  • 2007 - Drops "Computer" from Apple's name. Introduces the iPhone: "iPhone is a revolutionary and magical product that is literally five years ahead of any other mobile phone...We are all born with the ultimate pointing device--our fingers--and iPhone uses them to create the most revolutionary user interface since the mouse."
  • 2011 - January: Goes on medical leave of absence from CEO post nearly two years after the six-month break he took to undergo a liver transplant.
  • 2011 - June: Steve Jobs appears at the Cupertino City Council to unveil Apple's plans for its new 'Spaceship' campus. Which was his last public appearance.
  • 2011 - Steve Jobs dies at home, surrounded by his family
